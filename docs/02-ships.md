# Iron Exiles - Ships & Fleet Design

## Game Design Document: Ship Classes & Combat Capabilities

---

## Ship Classification System

Ships are classified by **size**, **role**, and **tech tier** (determined by faction).

### Size Classes

| Class | Crew Size | Length (approx) | Role |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|------|
| Fighter | 1-2 | 10-30m | Dogfighting, interception |
| Corvette | 5-15 | 50-100m | Patrol, recon, fast attack |
| Frigate | 30-80 | 150-300m | Escort, anti-fighter |
| Destroyer | 80-200 | 300-500m | Multi-role combat |
| Cruiser | 200-500 | 500-800m | Fleet backbone |
| Battlecruiser | 500-1000 | 800-1200m | Heavy assault |
| Battleship | 1000-3000 | 1200-2000m | Fleet command, siege |
| Dreadnought | 3000+ | 2000m+ | Capital ship, overwhelming force |
| Carrier | 500-2000 | 1000-2500m | Fighter/drone deployment |

## Ship Systems (Universal)

### Core Systems

| System | Function | Gameplay Impact |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Hull | Structural integrity | Base HP |
| Shields | Energy barrier | Regenerating HP layer |
| Reactor | Power generation | Powers all systems, allocation choices |
| Jump Drive | FTL travel | Cooldown-based, escape mechanic |
| Sensors | Detection range | Determines engagement awareness |
| Life Support | Crew survival | Damage here causes crew attrition |

### Weapon Categories

| Type | Characteristics | Best Against |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| Railguns/Kinetics | High velocity projectiles, ammo-limited | Hulls, unshielded targets |
| Energy Beams | Sustained damage, power-hungry | Shields |
| Plasma Weapons | Area damage, moderate range | Grouped enemies |
| Missiles/Torpedoes | Lock-on, counterable, high burst | All (if not intercepted) |
| Gravitic Weapons | Ignore shields partially | Heavily shielded targets |
| Point Defense | Anti-missile/fighter | Incoming projectiles |

### Defense Systems

| Type | Function |
|------|----------|
| Energy Shields | Absorb incoming damage, recharge over time |
| Ablative Armor | Reduces damage after shields fall |
| ECM/Countermeasures | Reduces enemy accuracy, breaks locks |
| Point Defense | Intercepts missiles and fighters |
| Stealth/Cloak | Prevents detection until attack or proximity |

3. **Engines** - Speed, maneuverability, jump cooldown

## Design Notes

- Ship combat should feel asymmetric and desperate, where cleverness beats raw power
- The player should always be outgunned on paper but win through tactics and Kestrel
- Fleet composition matters in larger battles (late game)
- Each faction's ships should be immediately recognizable by silhouette and combat behavior
- Upgrade scavenging creates a compelling exploration loop

### Scaling Notes

- **Jump Drive Range:** Base value is in "sectors per jump." Vorathis ships gain a +2 sector bonus from advanced navigation tech. Human player ships can push range beyond the baseline via Elder artifact upgrades late-game (up to +3 sectors for the Iron Wraith, up to +5 for Detroit-class).
- **Capacitor Recharge:** Time to fully recharge the jump drive capacitor after firing. Large ships have bigger capacitors that take longer to fill. A capacitor can also be partially recharged during combat by routing excess reactor power (at the cost of weapons/shields), allowing emergency "half-fire" after roughly half the listed time.
- **Beam Slots:** Energy beam turrets. Sustained damage, strongest against shields. No ammo -- draws from reactor power.
- **Railgun Slots:** Kinetic railguns. High burst damage against hulls. Ammo-limited (infinite for practical purposes in combat; resupply required between missions). Best early-game before shield tech catches up.
- **Missile Racks:** Lock-on missiles/torpedoes. Counterable by point defense and ECM. High burst, moderate range. All ships get at least 2 racks unless they are specialized EW platforms like the Hive Ship.
